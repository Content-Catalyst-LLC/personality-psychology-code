# Responsible Interpretation

Trait hierarchies require careful interpretation because greater granularity can create false confidence. A facet score may feel more precise than a domain score, but precision does not automatically mean validity, fairness, or interpretive depth.

A person cannot be reduced to a domain, facet, aspect, nuance, item response, profile chart, or hierarchical score. These measures describe patterns of response under particular measurement conditions. They do not exhaust identity, culture, life history, moral character, creativity, trauma, disability, spirituality, relationship context, or institutional position.

The level of measurement must match the level of interpretation. Broad scores should not be used to make narrow claims without evidence. Narrow scores should not be used to make broad claims without aggregation. Reliability, validity, invariance, and interpretability must be established at the level being used.

This scaffold is appropriate for education, research prototyping, psychometric demonstration, consulting support, organizational learning, coaching reflection, and methodological comparison. It is not a standalone system for hiring, promotion, termination, diagnosis, educational placement, legal evaluation, insurance decisions, surveillance, or individual prediction.
