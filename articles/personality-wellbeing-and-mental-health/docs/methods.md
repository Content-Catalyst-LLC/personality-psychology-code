# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, coping effectiveness, perceived support, stress burden, treatment access, sleep quality, age band, and life context interact to shape wellbeing, distress, flourishing, and social functioning?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates six levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — Big Five traits that shape emotional, social, and regulatory style.
2. **Pathway variables** — coping effectiveness, perceived support, stress burden, treatment access, and sleep quality.
3. **Wellbeing dimensions** — positive affect, negative affect, life satisfaction, meaning, and purpose.
4. **Two-continua outcomes** — distress and flourishing treated as related but distinct dimensions.
5. **Context** — age band and life context as conditions that shape trait expression.
6. **Functioning** — broad wellbeing and social functioning as lived outcomes.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Age-band summaries
- Life-context summaries
- Trait-wellbeing correlation matrices
- Regulation-support and flourishing-capacity indices
- Distress vulnerability indices
- Wellbeing models
- Distress models
- Flourishing models
- Two-continua profile summaries
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality and mental health should use validated instruments, careful sampling, transparent construct definitions, ethical data governance, and contextual variables. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a clinical, diagnostic, or psychological assessment system.

## Recommended extensions

- Longitudinal modeling of distress and flourishing
- Multilevel modeling by social context
- Mediation through coping and support
- Measurement invariance across groups
- Two-continua profile stability analysis
- Mixed-methods analysis of meaning and recovery
- Integration with non-sensitive public wellbeing datasets when appropriate
