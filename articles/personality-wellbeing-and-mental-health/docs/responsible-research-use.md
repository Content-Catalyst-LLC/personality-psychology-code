# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality, wellbeing, and mental health. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how traits, coping, support, stress burden, meaning, sleep, treatment access, and social context interact to shape distress and flourishing.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- personality and distress
- personality and wellbeing
- personality and flourishing
- positive affect and negative affect
- life satisfaction and meaning
- coping effectiveness and perceived support
- stress burden and vulnerability
- the two-continua view of mental health
- the difference between individual tendencies and structural conditions

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private mental-health data or implying claims about actual people, patients, communities, or care systems.

## Interpretation principles

Personality-wellbeing evidence should be interpreted through interaction. Apparent differences in distress, flourishing, coping, or support may reflect many sources, including:

- trauma history
- disability and chronic illness
- poverty and material insecurity
- discrimination and exclusion
- caregiving burden
- isolation and loneliness
- institutional violence
- family systems
- cultural norms
- sleep disruption
- treatment access
- social support
- measurement design

Personality language should not be used to blame people for suffering, pathologize ordinary difference, diagnose character, rank psychological worth, or reduce mental health to disposition. Structural language should not be used to ignore real differences in coping, self-regulation, meaning, and support-seeking. The strongest interpretation asks how persons and worlds shape each other.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- personality-wellbeing analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for diagnosis, treatment planning, clinical prediction, insurance scoring, workplace wellness surveillance, mental-health ranking, psychological assessment, or claims about actual people. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined wellbeing analysis: use personality models where they clarify, examine social context where it shapes opportunity, distinguish distress from flourishing, and treat suffering with dignity rather than reduction.
