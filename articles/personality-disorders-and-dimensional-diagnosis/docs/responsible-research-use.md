# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality disorders and dimensional diagnosis. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how severity, maladaptive traits, self-functioning impairment, interpersonal-functioning impairment, risk, treatment engagement, and context can be represented in dimensional models.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- dimensional personality disorder severity
- maladaptive trait domains
- identity and self-direction impairment
- empathy and intimacy impairment
- DSM-5 AMPD-style formulation
- ICD-11-style severity and trait qualifiers
- borderline pattern indicators
- risk and treatment engagement as separate clinical-planning constructs
- the distinction between clinical description and moral judgment

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private clinical data or implying claims about actual people, patients, clinicians, or treatment systems.

## Interpretation principles

Dimensional personality-disorder evidence should be interpreted through clinical context. Apparent differences in severity, functioning, trait expression, risk, or treatment engagement may reflect many sources, including:

- trauma history
- disability and neurodivergence
- mood disorders
- psychotic-spectrum conditions
- substance use
- medical conditions
- family systems
- social exclusion
- poverty and housing instability
- discrimination and stigma
- treatment access
- clinician bias
- cultural norms
- measurement design

Personality-disorder language should not be used casually, pejoratively, or as a substitute for qualified clinical assessment. It should not be used to label people as defective, untreatable, manipulative, dangerous, morally inferior, or reducible to a diagnostic identity.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- dimensional diagnosis analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for diagnosis, treatment planning, risk assessment, clinical decision support, hiring, workplace screening, custody decisions, legal judgments, insurance scoring, or claims about actual people. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined clinical-science analysis: use dimensional models where they clarify, separate severity from style, distinguish risk from diagnosis, preserve dignity, and treat personality functioning as clinically serious without reducing persons to labels.
