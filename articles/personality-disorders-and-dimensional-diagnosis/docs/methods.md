# Methods Notes

## Research question

How can dimensional personality-disorder diagnosis be modeled through severity, maladaptive trait domains, impairment in self and interpersonal functioning, risk indicators, treatment engagement, and clinical context?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates six levels of analysis:

1. **Maladaptive trait domains** — negative affectivity, detachment, antagonism, disinhibition, psychoticism, and anankastia.
2. **Self-functioning impairment** — identity and self-direction impairment.
3. **Interpersonal-functioning impairment** — empathy and intimacy impairment.
4. **Overall severity** — dimensional personality-disorder severity.
5. **Clinical-planning indicators** — risk level and treatment engagement.
6. **Context** — clinical context and perceived support as conditions that shape observed outcomes.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Severity-band summaries
- Dominant-trait-domain summaries
- Clinical-context summaries
- Trait and functioning correlations
- Dimensional severity models
- Severity x trait burden interaction models
- Risk-level models
- Treatment-engagement models
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality disorders should use validated measures, qualified clinical assessment, transparent sampling, careful differential diagnosis, cultural formulation, trauma-informed interpretation, and ethical governance of sensitive data. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a clinical diagnostic system.

## Recommended extensions

- Longitudinal modeling of personality functioning
- Measurement invariance across settings and cultures
- Clinician-rated versus self-report comparisons
- Treatment outcome modeling
- Risk and protective-factor models
- Crosswalks between AMPD and ICD-11 dimensional constructs
- Mixed-methods clinical formulation studies
