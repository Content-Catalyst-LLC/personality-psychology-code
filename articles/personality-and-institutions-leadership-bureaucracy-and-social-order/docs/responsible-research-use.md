# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality in institutional settings. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how traits, roles, accountability systems, discretion, and institutional contexts interact to shape leadership, performance, and trust.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- personality expression inside institutional roles
- role fit and bureaucratic conduct
- leadership, discretion, and accountability
- institutional trust and legitimacy
- personality risk under weak constraint
- stewardship, domination, procedural rigidity, and responsible judgment
- the difference between individual disposition and institutional design

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private personnel data or implying claims about actual organizations.

## Interpretation principles

Institutional personality evidence should be interpreted through interaction. Apparent differences in leadership or performance may reflect many sources, including:

- role expectations
- authority level
- discretion
- accountability systems
- organizational culture
- selection and promotion pressures
- resource constraints
- institutional incentives
- measurement design
- differences between formal rules and actual practice

Personality language should not be used to individualize structural failure. Structural language should not be used to excuse destructive conduct by officeholders. The strongest interpretation asks how institutions select, reward, amplify, restrain, or reform personality tendencies.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- institutional analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for personnel decisions, employee ranking, leadership selection, clinical assessment, workplace surveillance, or claims about actual organizations. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined institutional analysis: use personality models where they clarify, examine structure where it constrains, test interactions where they matter, and treat institutional conduct as the product of both character and office.
