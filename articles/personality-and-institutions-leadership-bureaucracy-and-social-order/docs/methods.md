# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, role fit, discretion, accountability, and institutional context interact to shape leadership, performance, and trust?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates five levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — trait patterns that shape judgment, reliability, social conduct, and risk.
2. **Role structure** — obligations, discretion, authority, and clarity attached to institutional positions.
3. **Institutional context** — units, bureaucratic routines, accountability systems, and organizational norms.
4. **Interaction effects** — how traits matter differently under different roles and constraints.
5. **Institutional outcomes** — trust, performance, leadership ratings, and risk.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Institutional-unit summaries
- Role-type summaries
- Trait correlation matrices
- Role-fit index construction
- Institutional-risk index construction
- Regression-style models of leadership, performance, and trust
- Simple discretion/accountability interaction logic
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality in institutions should use validated instruments, clear construct definitions, role analysis, institutional context documentation, and careful attention to power. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a personnel-assessment system.

## Recommended extensions

- Multilevel modeling by unit and role
- Longitudinal analysis of institutional trust
- Measurement validation for role-fit constructs
- Text analysis of institutional rules and decision memos
- Network analysis of authority and reporting structure
- Mixed-methods study of discretion and accountability
- Comparison between formal rules and actual practices
