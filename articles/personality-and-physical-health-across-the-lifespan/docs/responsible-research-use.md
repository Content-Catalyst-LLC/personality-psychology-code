# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality and physical health across the lifespan. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how traits, health behaviors, stress burden, support, adherence, age, and structural context interact to shape physical health and functional ability.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- personality and health behavior
- conscientiousness and preventive routine
- neuroticism, stress burden, and physiological vulnerability
- healthy neuroticism as a conditional configuration
- sleep, movement, substance risk, and adherence pathways
- social support and care access
- ageing, functional ability, and chronic-condition burden
- the difference between individual tendencies and structural health conditions

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private health data or implying claims about actual people, patients, communities, or health systems.

## Interpretation principles

Personality-health evidence should be interpreted through interaction. Apparent differences in physical health, functional ability, or adherence may reflect many sources, including:

- biology and genetics
- disability
- trauma history
- occupational hazard
- housing and transportation
- food systems
- pollution exposure
- poverty and economic strain
- discrimination and medical mistrust
- healthcare access
- caregiving burden
- public policy
- social support
- measurement design

Personality language should not be used to blame people for illness, disability, ageing, nonadherence, or health inequality. Structural language should not be used to ignore differences in coping, support, behavior, or self-regulation. The strongest interpretation asks how persons, bodies, environments, and institutions shape each other over time.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- lifespan personality-health analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for diagnosis, treatment, clinical prediction, insurance scoring, eligibility decisions, workplace wellness surveillance, population ranking, or claims about actual disease risk. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined lifespan analysis: use personality models where they clarify, examine health systems where they shape opportunity, test pathways where they matter, and treat illness and ageing with dignity rather than reduction.
