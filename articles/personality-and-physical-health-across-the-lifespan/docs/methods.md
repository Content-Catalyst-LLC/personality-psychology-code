# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, health behaviors, stress burden, social support, treatment adherence, age, and structural context interact to shape physical health, functional ability, and chronic-condition burden across the lifespan?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates six levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — Big Five traits and emotional stability.
2. **Health behavior** — exercise, sleep, smoking risk, alcohol risk, and medication adherence.
3. **Stress and support** — stress burden and perceived support.
4. **Life course** — age, age band, and repeated wave structure.
5. **Structural context** — life contexts that represent health opportunity and burden.
6. **Health outcomes** — physical health, functional ability, and chronic-condition burden.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Age-band summaries
- Life-context summaries
- Trait-health correlation matrices
- Health behavior index construction
- Stress vulnerability index construction
- Physical health models
- Functional ability models
- Healthy neuroticism interaction examples
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality and physical health should use validated instruments, ethically governed health data, transparent sampling, longitudinal design where possible, and careful treatment of social determinants of health. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a clinical or public-health prediction system.

## Recommended extensions

- Multilevel longitudinal modeling
- Mediation analysis through health behaviors
- Moderation by socioeconomic and care-access variables
- Healthy neuroticism interaction testing
- Functional ability models for older adults
- Chronic-condition burden models
- Measurement validation across age bands and cultures
- Integration with non-sensitive public-health datasets when appropriate
