# Responsible Interpretation

## Purpose

This repository supports professional education, research prototyping, consulting support, organizational learning, and methodological demonstration around self-report measurement, observer ratings, reliability, validity, self–other agreement, response quality, and psychometric interpretation.

## Data Notes

The dataset is synthetic. It exists so workflows can be inspected, replicated, and extended without relying on sensitive human-subject data. The patterns are constructed examples for testing measurement architecture, not claims about actual people, clients, students, workers, teams, organizations, schools, workplaces, cultures, or institutions.

## Interpretive Standards

A trait score is a model-based estimate, not the person. Self-report and observer-report data each carry information and limitations. Disagreement between them can reflect error, but it can also reveal meaningful differences between inner experience, self-concept, public behavior, reputation, role context, and social impact.

Reliability and validity are use-specific. A measure may be suitable for teaching or low-stakes reflection without being suitable for hiring, diagnosis, placement, or legal evaluation. Scores should not be compared across groups, methods, languages, or settings unless there is evidence that the measure functions comparably.

## Use Boundary

This scaffold is appropriate for analytic, educational, methodological, reflective, and professional-development purposes. It is not a standalone system for hiring, promotion, termination, clinical assessment, diagnosis, educational placement, legal evaluation, relationship matching, or individual prediction.
