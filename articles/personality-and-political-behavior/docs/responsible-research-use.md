# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality and political behavior. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how traits, political context, identity, efficacy, threat perception, media exposure, and civic opportunity interact to shape ideology, participation, affective polarization, institutional trust, and leadership preference.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- personality and political attitudes
- personality and political participation
- political interest and efficacy as engagement pathways
- group identity and perceived threat
- affective polarization
- institutional trust
- leadership-authority preference
- context-sensitive trait-politics relationships
- the difference between psychological style and political judgment

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private survey data or implying claims about actual political populations.

## Interpretation principles

Political personality evidence should be interpreted through interaction. Apparent differences in ideology, participation, threat perception, or polarization may reflect many sources, including:

- institutions
- party systems
- issue framing
- media environments
- social class
- race and ethnicity
- religion
- education
- geography
- historical memory
- group identity
- civic opportunity
- measurement design

Personality language should not be used to pathologize political disagreement. Political context should not be ignored when interpreting trait associations. The strongest interpretation asks how people with different psychological styles encounter different political worlds.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- political-behavior analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for voter targeting, psychological profiling, campaign manipulation, ideological diagnosis, population ranking, or claims about actual political groups. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined civic analysis: use personality models where they clarify, examine political context where it shapes meaning, test interactions where they matter, and treat political disagreement as a social and institutional phenomenon rather than a personality defect.
