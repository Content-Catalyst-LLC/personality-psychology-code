# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, political context, identity, efficacy, threat perception, media exposure, and civic opportunity interact to shape ideology, participation, affective polarization, leadership preference, and institutional trust?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates five levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — Big Five trait patterns that shape political style and orientation.
2. **Political context** — institutions, party systems, media environments, and civic opportunity.
3. **Political pathways** — interest, efficacy, identity, perceived threat, and mobilization.
4. **Interaction effects** — how traits matter differently across contexts and issue meanings.
5. **Political outcomes** — ideology, participation, affective polarization, institutional trust, and leadership preference.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Political-context summaries
- Trait correlation matrices
- Participation pathway models
- Ideology models with context sensitivity
- Affective-polarization models using identity and threat
- Institutional trust and leadership-authority preference summaries
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality and political behavior should use validated instruments, transparent sampling, careful causal identification, contextual political variables, and caution about self-report. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a voter-profiling or political-targeting system.

## Recommended extensions

- Multilevel modeling by country or political context
- Issue-domain-specific ideology models
- Longitudinal panel analysis
- Measurement-invariance testing across countries
- Media-cue and threat-exposure experiments
- Network analysis of civic mobilization
- Mixed-methods study of identity and political meaning
