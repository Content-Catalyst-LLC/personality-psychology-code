# Responsible Research Use

This repository supports reproducible analysis of personality, relationships, and social functioning. It is intended as a transparent research scaffold: a way to examine how traits, empathy, self-regulation, attachment security, support, loneliness, conflict, reciprocity, and social context interact to shape relational life.

## Research purpose

The central purpose of this repository is to support careful analysis of:

- personality and relationship satisfaction
- personality and broader social functioning
- empathy and self-regulation as relational capacities
- attachment security and perceived support
- loneliness, exclusion, and social breakdown
- conflict frequency and repair conditions
- reciprocity quality and reputation trust
- the difference between personal tendencies and social conditions

The included dataset is synthetic. It is designed to make the code reproducible and inspectable without relying on private relationship data or implying claims about actual people, couples, families, friendships, or communities.

## Interpretation principles

Relational-personality evidence should be interpreted through interaction. Apparent differences in satisfaction, functioning, support, loneliness, or conflict may reflect many sources, including:

- partner dynamics
- family systems
- attachment history
- trauma history
- disability and neurodivergence
- discrimination and exclusion
- poverty and economic strain
- caregiving burden
- grief and illness
- cultural norms
- social support
- current stress
- measurement design

Personality language should not be used to label people as relationally defective, toxic, doomed, needy, avoidant, or socially inferior. Structural language should not be used to excuse harmful conduct. The strongest interpretation asks how persons and social worlds shape each other over time.

## Appropriate use

This scaffold is appropriate for:

- reproducible research demonstrations
- educational workflows
- methodological prototyping
- synthetic-data testing
- relational-personality analysis examples
- article companion code
- transparent discussion of model limits

## Boundary of interpretation

The repository is not a basis for relationship diagnosis, couple assessment, family evaluation, clinical judgment, social ranking, compatibility scoring, or claims about actual people. Those uses would violate the methodological purpose of the project and misunderstand what synthetic-data workflows can support.

The intended standard is disciplined relational analysis: use personality models where they clarify, examine social context where it shapes meaning, distinguish outcomes carefully, and treat relationship difficulty with dignity rather than reduction.
