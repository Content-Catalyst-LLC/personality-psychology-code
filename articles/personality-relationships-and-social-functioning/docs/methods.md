# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, empathy, self-regulation, attachment security, perceived support, and social context interact to shape relationship satisfaction, social functioning, loneliness, conflict, reciprocity, and reputation trust?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates six levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — Big Five traits that shape relational style.
2. **Relational capacities** — empathy, self-regulation, attachment security, and perceived support.
3. **Relationship domain** — romance, friendship, family, work, community, and broader social context.
4. **Relational outcomes** — relationship satisfaction, reciprocity, conflict, loneliness, and social functioning.
5. **Social context** — support, exclusion, stress, culture, and environmental fit.
6. **Reputational outcomes** — trust, reliability, and social expectations over time.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Social-context summaries
- Relationship-domain summaries
- Trait correlation matrices
- Relational stability, support, and conflict-risk indices
- Relationship satisfaction models
- Social functioning models
- Loneliness and conflict models
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality and relationships should use validated instruments, dyadic or longitudinal design where possible, clear construct definitions, and careful treatment of social context. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a clinical or relationship-assessment system.

## Recommended extensions

- Actor-partner interdependence modeling
- Longitudinal relationship-quality models
- Network analysis of social functioning
- Multilevel models by social context
- Measurement validation across cultures
- Mixed-methods study of support and conflict
- Dynamic models of repair, trust, and rupture
