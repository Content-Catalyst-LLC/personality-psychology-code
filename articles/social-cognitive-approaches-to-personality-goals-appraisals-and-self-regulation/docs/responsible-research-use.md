# Responsible Research Use

This repository provides a transparent research scaffold for studying social-cognitive personality processes as person-in-context systems. Its purpose is to support reproducible method development, synthetic repeated-measures testing, if–then pattern summaries, and careful discussion of how goals, appraisals, expectancies, self-regulation, support, and behavior can be represented analytically.

The synthetic dataset is included so the workflow can be inspected, replicated, and extended without relying on sensitive human-subject data. Its patterns are constructed examples for testing model architecture, not empirical claims about actual people, students, patients, workers, classrooms, families, organizations, or communities.

The interpretive standard is disciplined and contextual. Goals, appraisals, expectancies, and self-regulation are not complete explanations of a person. Low self-efficacy, avoidance, threat appraisal, or poor regulation may reflect trauma history, exclusion, disability, poverty, discrimination, unsafe institutions, blocked opportunity, poor instruction, social threat, or repeated experiences of powerlessness.

This scaffold is best used to clarify concepts, test reproducible workflows, and support careful research design. It preserves the distinction between methodological modeling and assessment of persons while treating personality as a dynamic system shaped by development, culture, relationships, institutions, and available support.
