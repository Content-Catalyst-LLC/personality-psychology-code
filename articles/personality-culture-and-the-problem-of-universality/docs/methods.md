# Methods Notes

## Research question

How should personality psychology evaluate the claim that broad trait structures are universal across cultures?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates four levels of analysis:

1. **Trait description** — whether people describe enduring individual differences.
2. **Broad recurrence** — whether high-level trait domains recur across groups.
3. **Measurement comparability** — whether scales and items function similarly across groups.
4. **Behavioral manifestation** — whether traits are expressed through culturally specific norms and roles.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Group-level descriptive summaries
- Within-group correlation matrices
- Matrix-similarity comparison
- Simple trait-context interaction modeling
- Big Five plus Honesty-Humility comparison
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional cross-cultural analysis should use item-level data, translation documentation, response-style checks, and formal measurement-invariance testing. The scripts here provide educational scaffolding, not a substitute for a full psychometric study.

## Recommended extensions

- Multi-group confirmatory factor analysis
- Exploratory structural equation modeling
- Item response theory across groups
- Response-style correction
- Local lexical analysis
- Qualitative validation of trait meanings
- Mixed-methods comparison of behavioral manifestations
