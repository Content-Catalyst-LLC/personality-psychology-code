# Methods Notes

This scaffold models behavior-genetic personality analysis through linked dimensions:

1. monozygotic and dizygotic twin-style resemblance
2. trait, temperament-reactivity, and effortful-control outcomes
3. rough ACE-style variance estimates
4. bootstrap uncertainty around simplified estimates
5. shared environmental indicators
6. nonshared environmental indicators
7. environmental moderation of twin differences
8. gene–environment interaction concepts
9. gene–environment correlation concepts
10. developmental-context interpretation
11. professional-use boundaries and responsible interpretation

The workflow is designed for transparent method demonstration. A real behavior-genetic study would require validated measures, formal structural equation modeling, adequate sampling, twin-model assumption checks, missing-data planning, ancestry/population-structure care where genomic data are involved, informed consent, privacy safeguards, genetic-data governance, anti-discrimination safeguards, and appropriate ethical review.

The simplified formulas here are teaching tools. They do not replace full ACE/ADE/AE/CE model fitting, genomic analysis, or qualified behavior-genetic interpretation.
