# Responsible Interpretation

## Purpose

This repository supports professional education, research prototyping, consulting support, organizational learning, and methodological demonstration around behavior genetics, personality heritability, twin-style analysis, variance decomposition, gene–environment interplay, and developmental interpretation.

## Data Notes

The dataset is synthetic. It exists so workflows can be inspected, replicated, and extended without relying on sensitive human-subject data. The patterns are constructed examples for testing model architecture, not claims about actual people, twins, families, genetic groups, clients, students, workers, teams, organizations, schools, workplaces, cultures, or institutions.

## Interpretive Standards

A heritability estimate is a population-level statistic under specific conditions. It is not the proportion of an individual person caused by genes. It is not proof of immutability. It is not a moral ranking. It is not a substitute for developmental explanation.

Genetic influence should be interpreted as probabilistic, polygenic, context-sensitive, and developmentally mediated. Environmental influence should be interpreted seriously, including shared environment, nonshared environment, social inequality, institutional conditions, peer relations, stress, support, and life-course timing.

## Use Boundary

This scaffold is appropriate for analytic, educational, methodological, reflective, and professional-development purposes. It is not a standalone system for hiring, promotion, termination, clinical diagnosis, educational placement, legal evaluation, insurance decisions, relationship matching, genetic screening, surveillance, or individual prediction.
