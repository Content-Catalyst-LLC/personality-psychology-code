# Responsible Interpretation

Alternative structural models require careful interpretation because structural refinement can create false confidence. HEXACO, hierarchy, facets, circumplexes, and networks can improve personality science, but they can also be misused if their scores are treated as verdicts on a person rather than as measured indicators within a specific model.

A person cannot be reduced to a Big Five profile, a HEXACO score, a Honesty-Humility estimate, a facet pattern, a circumplex location, a network node, or a factor loading. These tools describe patterns of response, behavior, or judgment under particular measurement conditions. They do not exhaust identity, culture, life history, moral character, trauma, disability, spirituality, relational context, institutional position, or future possibility.

Honesty-Humility is important, but it is not a moral sentence. A score cannot prove honesty, dishonesty, corruption, exploitation, virtue, or character. It can support probabilistic research and careful interpretation, but it should not be used as a shortcut for judging people.

This scaffold is appropriate for professional education, research prototyping, psychometric demonstration, consulting support, organizational learning, coaching reflection, and methodological comparison. It is not a standalone system for hiring, promotion, termination, diagnosis, educational placement, legal evaluation, insurance decisions, surveillance, relationship matching, moral labeling, or individual prediction.
