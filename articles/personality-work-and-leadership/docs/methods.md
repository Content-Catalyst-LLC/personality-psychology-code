# Methods Notes

## Research question

How do personality traits, role fit, organizational context, and accountability interact to shape job performance, teamwork, leadership emergence, leadership effectiveness, counterproductive workplace behavior, and burnout risk?

## Analytic frame

This scaffold separates six levels of analysis:

1. **Personality profile** — Big Five traits, emotional stability, and dark-trait pressure.
2. **Role structure** — role family, role demands, and person-role fit.
3. **Organizational context** — culture, reward systems, accountability, and institutional constraint.
4. **Work outcomes** — job performance, teamwork, and burnout risk.
5. **Leadership outcomes** — leadership emergence and leadership effectiveness.
6. **Risk outcomes** — counterproductive workplace behavior and derailment risk.

## Example analyses

The included scripts demonstrate:

- Role-family summaries
- Organizational-context summaries
- Trait correlation matrices
- Dependability, stewardship, and derailment-risk indices
- Performance and leadership models
- Counterproductive workplace behavior models
- Output generation for reproducibility

## Measurement note

A professional study of personality at work should use validated instruments, ethical data governance, transparent construct definitions, role analysis, and careful attention to power. This repository provides educational scaffolding, not a workplace-selection or employee-assessment system.

## Recommended extensions

- Multilevel modeling by team or organization
- Longitudinal performance and leadership data
- Measurement validation for role-fit constructs
- Role-family-specific model comparison
- Team-network analysis
- Accountability and culture moderation models
- Mixed-methods study of leadership effectiveness and derailment
