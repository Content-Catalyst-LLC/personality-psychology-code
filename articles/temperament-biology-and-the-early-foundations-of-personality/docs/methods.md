# Methods Notes

This scaffold models temperament and later personality through linked developmental dimensions: early inhibition, negative affectivity, surgency, effortful control, caregiving support, family stress, classroom support, peer support, institutional stability, later conscientiousness, neuroticism, social confidence, regulation skill, reactivity-regulation balance, environmental support, developmental risk, and adaptive pathway scoring.

A real developmental study would require validated instruments, longitudinal design, child/family consent procedures, institutional review, missing-data planning, developmental-stage appropriateness, cultural validity, informant strategy, privacy safeguards, and careful communication of uncertainty.

The models here are teaching and prototyping tools. They do not replace qualified developmental, clinical, educational, or psychometric assessment.
